The Fountainhead

My Heel

I’ve always known what my Achilles heel is.

Over the last few years I’ve grown distant from people. I’ve avoided making introductions, reaching out, etc.

I’ve had my justifications: “I’m working on something,” “I don’t want to compromise myself to try to make an impression on them,” and similar thoughts come to mind.

The true reason was always twofold. First, elitism. I believed that no one could offer me anything new, and they were just wasting my time. Second, fear. Fear that they’ll see through me, they’ll see that I’m a fraud.

It comes to me in my weakest moments, this fear. It’s simple, slipstream.

Knowledge

There’s an old adage, well-known:

“It’s not what you know, but who you know!” –Everyone

I always hated it, fought against it violently. A new idea happened upon me recently that recontextualized my fight:

Who you know is what you know.

You are the amalgamation of the people you’re closest to. That much is clear. For me, however, I don’t spend that much time with people. Like I said, I generally stick to myself, focusing on my craft.

What does that mean for what I know?

The Fountainhead

is a book by Ayn Rand which I read recently. It crushed me.

Its portrayal of two characters trapped in the drama of all creation cut straight to the heart of my fears.

Howard Roark is an architect, an uncompromising artist – the ideal man. He works for the sake of product and practice, to glorify his own life force, and to exert it onto the world. He relaxes completely when he doesn’t work, experiencing the sensation of life as a pure stream of idea.

Peter Keating is a parasite, a social king – everything wrong with business. He acts in accordance with society’s expectations, never fulfills his own artistic desires (if he can even recognize such an instinct in himself), and lives miserably, caught between desire to become his own man and the crushing weight of social pressure.

I am Peter Keating.

In moments of fleeting vision, I transcend to become Howard Roark. Like some Steppenwolf of two faces, I try to justify myself as Roark most of the time, but it’s a rare form of expression. Usually it occurs during the process of creation. When making a movie, game, or blog post. As for Roark’s complete relaxation, his momentary bliss, almost never.

Reconciliation

Obviously, it’s a contradiction.

The question being: which is true? Which is right?

For you, I have nothing. It’s up to you.

For myself, I have an answer.

I will always follow that which is the more eternal, the more personal. The one that, in the vision of Jon Blow, expresses the subjective reality of my experience.

So I’m doubling down on my intentions. I seek pure creation. I will not seek people to get ahead. I will seek people in earnest, looking for ideas and companions seeking the same goal I seek.

In order to enforce this, I will despise that which is weak and pitiful in myself. I will not seek out help from others without first practicing the act of thinking and overcoming.
