# Abstract
Violence is misrepresented and misunderstood.

In films, stories, games, and even in the general public consciousness, what we call "violence" is a comical echo of anything real.

Try to imagine yourself in a scenario where your life is on the line. Where a single flash of cold steel could mean the end of everything you have ever accomplished.
Think of the terror of knowing you're outnumbered, even as a skilled fighter. Think even of the terror of just encountering one unpredictable foe!

Violence isn't numbers to be optimized, it's a sequence of real, consequential decisions.
It's so consequential that all but the most insane, desperate, brave, or honorable of mankind partake in it. The reasonable folk would turn tail and flee from something so insane.

This is the kind of simple drama I seek to create with this game.

I actually think that real-life violence is a far more rich and compelling subject than the dance of violence which our media focuses on.

I think we can make a really compelling game about how violence really is.
I want to give it a real, earnest effort. I want us to explore it the best we can.

# Premise
I propose a series of short games, exploring a variety of topics and characters.
Each game will be 1-3 sessions. I like to call this *Motion Picture Role-Playing*.
The games will explore the myriad forms which violence can take. It will also explore an extremely large variety of other topics as secondary focuses.

I'll run each game for anywhere between one to three players, depending on each game's premise and organization.
Each game will be conceptualized by the entire group, a week in advance of its start date. Anyone can come with an idea they'd like to explore, and we'll choose the most exciting ones to do next.
Most of the time, we'll plan the next game at the table after we finish the prior game. We'll keep a shared page for ideas for the next game which anyone can add to at any time.

The games take place in a single setting, in vicinity to one another, with games which refer to or stem from earlier games.
Here's an example of this:
> In the midst of a long, drawn-out war, a great castle has fallen under siege.
> - The first game follows two street-rat thieves, starving, penniless, looking for some way to survive in the infested sewers below the castle, and who come upon a mysterious secret.
> - The next game follows two soldiers in service of the besieging army. They hatch an insane scheme to breach the walls of the city, through the dank sewers underneath, and succeed!
> - The next game follows the last remaining members of the defending nation's Royal Guard, who lay down their lives to protect the princess and smuggle her safely out of the castle.
> - After that, we run a game deep in the northern woods, totally unrelated to this war, in which the characters encounter a mysterious traveler, who speaks in a melancholic high-born way. She tells the tale of how she is the last of her people.

# Inspirations
My inspirations are the following. I want to run a game which is:
- fast-paced, fantastical, but grounded, like *Conan, the Barbarian*.
- raw, gritty, but sensitive, like *First Blood* or *Yojimbo*.
- reverent, interconnected, and personal, like *The Dying Earth* or *Game of Thrones*.

You should bring your own inspirations to the table, too!
Your favorite film, book, character... Everything goes in to this game.
Since we iterate so quickly, running a new game every week or three, we can try anything which excites us!
