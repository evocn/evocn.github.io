Introduction

Hi there, I’m Alex. This is my first post.

The web has been so wonderful to me in my life (though I spend little time here now).

I love old internet homepages. here are a couple of examples. There’s something so cute about how raw these pages are, and the way that personal things are shared so effortlessly, without fear. They seem to have had some confidence that we all forgot.

When looking at these sites, I particularly love the jack-of-all-trades thing these hackers have going. Almost all of these guys post their poetry, their music, their life’s story, and all of their favorite things. It’s like everything these people are is collected in this one place.

In addition, they usually posts pictures. Each of these pictures is always so sublime, both aesthetically and in what they represent – candid shots of everyday life.

Today, I join their ranks.

Me

I play the piano, to a pretty serious degree when I was younger. Now I make music in Logic for fun and try out different ideas I have. What a wonderful time to be alive, when all the sounds of the world are available to me – to any of us!

I make short movies, since that is also an incredibly accessible medium now. I filmed a ~25 minute short last summer with some friends that I’ll post here when it’s finished, and a couple of music videos shot recently. Those are my favorite medium of film, because music and visuals go so well together.

Derek Sivers, Josh Waitzkin, and Jon Blow each have inspired me deeply. These three take life very seriously, stay curious, and work competitively, striving to understand fully. They are my role models, and different aspects of each speaks to different goals I have in my life.

My favorite musicians (in no particular order) are Leonard Cohen, Yoko Kanno, Labi Siffre, The Mountain Goats, Billy Joel, Paul Simon, Father John Misty, Jeffrey Lewis, Dion, Haruomi Hosono, Jonathan Richman, Lamp, Glen Campbell, John Prine, Car Seat Headrest, Bach, Mozart, Jim Croce, and The Seatbelts!

My favorite movies are hard to tie down, but I really loved The End of Evangelion once, when it was closer to me. The last really good movie I watched was probably The Wind Rises, which made me cry tears of joy! Never had that happen before, so I’m looking for more movies that can do that.

I’ve been reading books a lot more recently, mostly self-help books. I love Nietzsche, Steinbeck, Hesse, and a few others, but I’m just getting more into it recently, so I’m looking forward to reading broadly.
