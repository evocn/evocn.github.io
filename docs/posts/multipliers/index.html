Multipliers

Life is finite. How do you do the most you possibly can while you’re alive?

Not in an inhuman, mechanical sense – doing as much as possible in pursuit of as deeply intricate a life as possible. In order to pursue more aims, faster, with less resistance, towards whatever you desire.

As human beings, putting away hours every day, you can take two approaches.

The first is additive. Every day, put away the hours. This results in a wonderful life. Much better than not putting anything away at all.

The second, which is the subject of my interest here, is multiplicative. We know this mode well. You call them “10x engineers”, “life hackers”, and other silly names.

It’s not my intention to earn a silly name, but I do want to know how my time can multiply, rather than add.

Definition

A Multiplier is an aspect of life that multiplies other things.

  • Multipliers can multiply eachother, resulting in extreme efficiency.

Multipliers are the natural mode of growth for most things. A tree will go from one leaf to two leaves and then from two leaves to four leaves in the same amount of time. Your first three months programming will see you grawing a very small amount relative to the growth you experience in three months at expert-level.

Catalogue

Here’s a list of my favorite multipliers, which act as a foundation to allow me to do all the things that I do.

Willpower

  • Willpower is limited. Those among us perceived to have the strongest wills actually create systems to avoid using their willpower. It’s the reason why monks live in isolated locations where temptation will not come.
  • Zero-Effort Habits save your willpower in much the same way.
    • Disallow rules in a system, rather than discouraging them. It is hard to cheat at soccer, but it is impossible to cheat in the FIFA games.
    • It must take willpower to not do a habit, such that you will inevitably do it.

Time

Efficiency is often discussed at length in life-hack blogs of this sort, so I’ll disregard it mostly. Just remember to treat it as an investment, and if it won’t outlive your initial investment, it’s not worth doing it.

i.e.: stop optimizing, do the work.

It is clear, however, that those who can do their jobs in half the time earn half the time back. Focus on areas where you can get tangible results here.

Energy

  • Healthy, nourishing food will act as a multiplier for your energy.
  • Cold showers are intensely multiplicative. They transform my day every single time.
  • Running has about the same effect as a cold shower: total reset.
  • Good sleep multiplies about every single thing in my life.

Growth

  • learning early in life and doing work that pushes your limits results in compounding gains over the course of your lifetime.
    • This applies to art, programming, thought, and any craft.
  • Spaced Repetition is something I have not fully tested, but in many peoples’ experience acts as a multiplier of learning efficacy.
    • My own experience with this is in piano practice, in which the same four hours of practice spread over two weeks is far more effective than squeezed into one or two days.
  • Working on mental models and gaining new perspective will literally change the thoughts that your mind can have.

“Ideas are like fish. If you want to catch little fish, you can stay in the shallow water. But if you want to catch the big fish, you’ve got to go deeper.” – David Lynch, Catching the Big Fish

Miscellaneous

  • Money is multiplied in other countries.
    • If you
  • Being excited about your work is a multiplier.
    • When excited or curious, I find I can work from morning until night, entranced, without tiring. When I’m not, I have trouble getting any work at all done.

As you can see, I’ve found quite a few. I love multipliers, and I believe everyone who has accomplished something remarkable utilizes more multipliers than adders.

Find your own, and get multiplying!
