Core Rules

The Core Rule

This is a 2D6-based game. Everything in the game involves rolling 2D6, adding a given modifier to the roll, and testing that result against some Difficulty Class (DC).

Difficulty DC
Easy 5
Moderate 8
Hard 12
Impossible 18

Rolling Philosophy

I tend to resolve most things without dice when characters are proficient, have a significant advantage or good plan, or when they are not under any particular pressure.

Consequences

In a lot of circumstances, a roll has consequences. These rolls prioritizing failing forward, making interesting choices, and having more compelling decisions to make. The GM will inform the players beforehand of a roll’s consequences and how they are triggered.

For example…

When leaping a chasm to flee from a charging rhinoceros, the GM informs the player their roll will have consequences. On a 10, they will succeed without incident, bounding over the gap. On an 8 or 9, they will succeed, but take a wound as their leg cracks on the other side. On a 7 or lower, they will fail to grab the edge, but might have a chance to grasp at the roots hanging out of the chasm.

The purpose of the consequence is to make for a more interesting choice. Maybe this character’s on their last wound, and they have to make the choice to stand and fight. Maybe they’re willing to leave their Stowed equipment behind to give themselves a little extra agility to make the jump with certainty! It’s all for the sake of making the choices interesting.

Attributes

There are 6 attributes which govern characters, which typically range from -3 to +3.

Attribute Examples
Agility Dodging, sneaking, leaping.
Strength Pushing, pulling, climbing, wrestling, melee weapons.
Clarity Perception, ranged weapons.
Knowledge Lore, survival, mending of injuries, use of technology.
Grit Ability to withstand pain and stay alive when injured.

Negotiation

A lot of the time, actions can be governed by various attributes. I tend to work on a system where a player can negotiate the use of any attribute, and the GM makes a ruling.

Talents

Talents represent a character’s learned ability to perform some specific task.
