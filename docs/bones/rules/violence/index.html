Violence

This game is about Violence. The rules are simple, but consequential.

Combat

Combat happens in rounds. There isn’t initiative, we just go around the table in order, then the enemies go.

Each round, you can:

  • Move.
  • Do an Action.

There’s just a few combat-specific terms to know about in Bones:

  • Wounds governs how many wounds you can take before passing out. It is governed by Grit.
  • Avoid is how well you can avoid blows. It is governed by Agility.
  • Armor subtracts from damage dealt to you. It is governed by your Equipment.

Attacking

To Hit:

Roll 2D6. Add Strength to melee weapons, Add Clarity to ranged weapons. If the total meets the enemy’s Avoid, you hit!

Damage

In this game, weapons pose a real threat to your body. Every blow could break your arm, crack your skull, puncture your lung, or leave you with a flesh wound which will kill you in an hour.

In service of that, we don’t track Hit Points, instead we track Wounds. When something would harm you, like an arrow or a falling boulder, subtract your Armor from the Damage, then determine the Wounds inflicted:

Damage Wound
0-3 None
4-7 1
8+ 2

We roll on this table when a character takes a wound:

(D10) Wound Effect
0 Death’s Door Re-roll on this chart. All further Wounds are fatal until your next rest.
1 Severe Wound Re-roll on this chart. That injury is permanent. For instance, a Broken Arm is actually severed.
2-3 Broken Arm -2 Strength. -1 Hand.
4-5 Broken Leg -2 Agility. Half speed.
6-7 Brain Damage -2 to Knowledge, Clarity.
8-9 Deep Gash -1 to Strength, Agility.

When a character takes more Wounds than they can handle (Grit + 2), they pass out. They can be carried to safety and stabilized. An unconscious character who takes another Wound dies.

Recovery

Characters who have time and resources to recover can attempt to mend their Wounds. The GM determines when an appropriate time for the characters to get some rest is.

Each rest, once for each Minor Wound, any character can roll a DC 8 Knowledge check to mend a Wound. Alternatively, for your own wounds, you can attempt a DC 10 Grit check to manage a wound yourself.

If your character gets proper rest for two weeks, all Minor Injuries are healed.

Critical

On a weapon’s maximum damage output, you score a critical! You roll damage again. This can continue indefinitely!

Maneuvers

When you do something in a battle which isn’t an attack, it’s called a Maneuver. Classes have unique maneuvers, but here’s a list of maneuvers anyone can use:

Dodge

Give yourself +1 Armor and +1 Agility till next turn.

Defend

Take all attacks targeting a nearby ally.

Grab

Strength contest with an opponent. They can choose Strength or Agility. If you succeed, you and the opponent can’t move or take actions while you wrestle.

Dash

Use your movement again.

Shove

with your hands free, you can attempt to shove an opponent. You make an attack against their Avoid, and if you succeed, you push them 5 feet.
