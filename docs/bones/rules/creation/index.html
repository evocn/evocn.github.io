Rules for Character Creation

Character creation should be super quick. There’s just 5 steps. I think the hardest part should be coming up with a name.

Decide on a premise

This part happens at the table, with everyone present! Do this part together, and once everyone’s done, you can all move on to the next part.

Since Bones is a Motion Picture, not a tv show, we want characters who have a simple question to answer. Start from the premise that the group decided on. For instance…

We’re telling a War Story. Which characters can we imagine in that Movie?

  • The conscript, drafted the day after his sixteenth birthday.
  • The nameless warrior, who has known nothing but bloodshed since the day he set foot in this world.
  • The grizzled commanding officer, who has a haunting memory which keeps him from sleeping at night.
  • The prisoner of war, living in an uncertain stasis between life and death.
  • The deserter, who left for righteous reasons, but who can’t shake the feeling of guilt.
  • The lone survivor of a battle, who feels it is his duty to live the best life he can for those who died for him.
  • The daughter of an honorable family, secretly taking her father’s place to protect his life, at risk of her family’s honor and her own life.

Choose an archetype, a story, a conflict, or just something familiar. Talk with the DM and the other player(s) to find a good combination of characters. The best groups are those where every character has some way to relate to the other characters, either through a conflict in beliefs, or through some deep connection which binds them together.

It’s good to know some things about your character, like their past, their beliefs, and it’s even good to know a little hint of where they’ll develop during the game, but don’t think too much further than that. The real magic of who your character is will reveal itself during the game!

Once the group has all come to a good feeling about who the characters of our game will be, everyone can move on to the rest of character creation individually.

Fill out your Sheet!

The rest is just choosing mechanical details that fit your character’s premise.

Choose their Attributes.

Distribute [+2, +1, 0, -1, -2] among your attributes however you see fit:

  • Agility
  • Strength
  • Clarity
  • Knowledge
  • Grit

Choose their Talents.

Remember Talents from the Core rules? These are specific activities or expertises, like climbing or history or persuasion. When you make a check and you’re Talented at it, you re-roll 1s on your initial roll.

Each character gets two Talents by default.

Select their Equipment.

Choose whatever you like from the Equipment page.

Remember how carrying works in this game. You can carry:

  • Items in both hands.
  • Two items attached to your body, at -1 Agility penalty each.
  • A suit of Armor.
  • One Pack.
  • A trinket.

Choose equipment which fits your character’s premise, and which you think would be interesting to use.

Calculate your Combat Stats.

  • Wound Capacity = 2 + Grit.
  • Avoid = 4 + Agility.
  • Armor = Add up your equipment’s bonuses.

That’s it! Depending on the game you’re making this character for, the GM might add some extra details, but this is the main chunk of it.
