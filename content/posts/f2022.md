Dear Reader,

So the year's comin' to an end. Thought I'd look back and check in on what all
I've been up to this year.

It's a long time, a year. That's 365 days. A single day seems small in the
scheme of things, but they sure do add up.

For the first part of the year, I rid myself of the concept of "dates", and set
all my calendars to just display the day of the year it was. For instance, today
is day 361. (I've got a little bash script to tell me if I ever get confused).

It was pretty funny to think about time. You'd look at the dates and think "man,
it's only day fifty, the year is still young! And on days like today, when you
really consider the fact that three-hundred and sixty days passed me by this
year, it's pretty hard to swallow. Especially because every single day this year
I got up and saw that it was its own day. I saw that number, in its stark
uniqueness, and lived it through. All of it.

I could ruminate on this all day, but I figured I'd chart out the way my year
went for posterity, and maybe review some of my favorite things from this year.

## jan – march
First three months were spent in San Luis Obispo, in a complete haze of
programming and music-making.

It was my first time spending such a long period alone. It harrowed me!

It was this time that, while resulting in very little, set the stage for the
changes that came throughout the rest of the year. It was then that I dedicated
myself to creation, to programming.

I didn't document my thoughts until this most recent fall, so it's hard to say
what I was into, what I felt.

I wrote three albums in four weekends with my friend Bryce. I wrote a song a day
for almost a hundred days.

But I couldn't program yet. It was slow going. I hadn't made the necessary
preparations against resistance. I was still young.

During this time I also started practicing BJJ, which was very hard at first.

## april – june
This was a fun time. I went back to school, took some of the most agreeable
courses I ever took here, and made the most of everything.

I spent my days learning, playing ultimate frisbee, and line dancing.
Although, in all the joy and excitement, I did forget the part of me that made
music and programmed all the time.

So the days passed, and by the summer time I had a great feeling – like
something had to be coming soon.

## july – sept
Ah, the summer!

It was quintessential. I swam every single day, filmed a movie, programmed a
heck of a lot, spent many a lazy day with June, and really thought hard about
what I wanted to do next.

I also rolled BJJ in my buddy Jordan's backyard, some of the most fun martial
arts I've ever had the privilege of enjoying.

It was this time that saw me starting to really put systems into place. I record
my thoughts now in a journal. I write my thoughts on every movie I see, every
book I read. It was around this time that I finally started to see the changes I had started to desire.

I also started a Dungeons and Dragons campaign, *Oblivion*, which has been a
near-complete success since the beginning. It's taken up a lot of time to prep
and run, but it's been engaging and interesting to design for and test new
theories on.

## oct – dec
This what when it all came together.

With the backdrop of some useless and frankly terrible classes to fill the
day-to-day, I set forth to become what I ought to be this quarter.

I started by reading a lot, then watching a lot of films, then making my own,
then by finally getting back into programming after a few months of
introspection on the topic.

I also started this blog! It was rough at first, but now I really enjoy tweaking
things, writing down ideas and summaries of my progress, and explaining things
in pursuit of understanding.

## Wrap up
So there's the year. I loved it, the best year yet.

I changed more this year than I think I ever have before.
I wonder how long this pattern can keep up.

Will there come a day when I reflect on a year, and I know in my heart I just
got worse? Or stayed the same?

I wonder what that will mean, when it comes.
