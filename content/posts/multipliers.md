Life is finite. How do you do the most you possibly can while you're alive?

Not in an inhuman, mechanical sense – doing as much as possible in pursuit of as
deeply intricate a life as possible. In order to pursue more aims, faster, with
less resistance, towards whatever you desire.

As human beings, putting away hours every day, you can take two approaches.

The first is additive. Every day, put away the hours. This results in a
wonderful life. Much better than not putting anything away at all.

The second, which is the subject of my interest here, is **multiplicative**.
We know this mode well. You call them "life hackers", and other silly names.

It's not my intention to earn a silly name, but I do want to know how my time
can multiply, rather than add.

### Definition
> A **Multiplier** is an aspect of life that multiplies other things.
* Multipliers can multiply eachother, resulting in extreme efficiency.

Multipliers are the natural mode of growth for most things.

A tree will go from one leaf to two leaves and then from two leaves to four
leaves in the same amount of time.

Your first three months programming will see you grawing a very small amount
relative to the growth you experience in three months at expert-level.

## Catalogue
Here's a list of my favorite multipliers, which act as a foundation to allow me
to do all the things that I do.

### Willpower
* **Create Systems** | Willpower is extremely limited. Those among us perceived
  to have the strongest wills actually ***create systems*** to avoid using their
  willpower. This is the reason why monks live in isolated locations where
  temptation will not create such resistance.
* **Zero-Effort Habits** | save your willpower in much the same way.
  * Disallow rules in a system, rather than discouraging them. It is hard to cheat
    at soccer, but it is impossible to cheat in the FIFA games.
  * It must take willpower to *not do* a habit, such that you will inevitably do
    it.

### Time
**Productivity**
  * Try to find optimum paths. 
  * Discover how much faster you really could do something if you tried.
  * Everything is faster when you're in [your territory](/posts/warofart).

> **Productivity Note:**
> 
> Often productivity gurus get obsessed with this topic. They spend more time
> optimizing than actually doing tasks that need doing. It's distraction –
> productivity theater.
> 
> Treat time spent optimizing your life as an investment, and if it won't
> outlive your initial investment, it's not worth doing it.
> 
> Stop optimizing – **do the work**.

It is clear, however, that those who can do their jobs in half the time earn
half the time back. Focus on areas where you can get tangible results here.

### Energy
* Healthy, nourishing **food** will act as a multiplier for your energy.
* **Cold showers** are intensely multiplicative. They transform my day every single
time.
* **Running** has about the same effect as a cold shower: total reset.
* Good **sleep** multiplies about every single thing in life.

When you fall into a period of low energy, consider the effect this will have
on your work. You might be able to force yourself through to the end of a task,
but you will take twice as long as if you had a high-energy state.

Worse still, low-energy states seem to compound on each other, wearing you down
week by week until it's hard to even get a little bit of energy going.

For these reasons, I always look for a **reset** of some kind.
If that reset means taking a nap and putting off work and you can genuinely
affirm its positive intention, go ahead.

Note that very rarely is Youtube a positive reset, and all media consumption
during a period of reset must be surveyed carefully, lest they worsen your
energy crisis.

### Growth
* **Learning early** in life and doing work that pushes your limits results in
  compounding gains over the course of your lifetime.
  * This applies to art, programming, thought, and any craft.
* **Spaced repetition** acts as a multiplier of learning efficacy.
  * My own experience with this is in piano practice, in which the same four
    hours of practice spread over two weeks is far more effective than squeezed
    into one or two days. It's actually astonishing how much of a difference
    this has made recently.
* **Mental models** are essential to growth in thought. Many people don't
  acknowledge that gaining new perspectives will literally change the thoughts
  that your mind is capable of having.

> "Ideas are like fish. If you want to catch little fish, you can stay in the
> shallow water. But if you want to catch the big fish, you've got to go
> deeper."
> – David Lynch, *Catching the Big Fish*

### Miscellaneous
* **Money** is multiplied in cheaper areas. I don't have too much experience
  with this, but it seems to be pretty popular these days. Figure I'll check out
  how good a multiplier it really is when I start looking at where to settle
  down after school.
* **Excitement** about your work is a multiplier.
  * When excited or curious, I find I can work from morning until night,
    entranced, without tiring. When I'm not, I have trouble getting any work at
    all done. This is one of those magical states which I seek above all else,
    since it renders most other optimizations irrelevant due to its sheer
    force.
