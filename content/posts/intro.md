Hi there, I'm Alex.
This is my first post.

The web has been so wonderful to me in my life (though I spend little time here now).

I love old internet homepages particularly.
[Here](http://number-none.com/blow/index.html) are a 
[couple](http://nothings.org/) of
[examples](https://web.archive.org/web/20131011125002/http://www-formal.stanford.edu/jmc/).
There's something so sublime about how raw these pages are, and
the way that stupid things are shared so effortlessly, without fear.
I sometimes wonder if these pages were even built knowing that this would be a
person's public-facing self, or if maybe that idea hadn't fully dawned on anyone
back in the day.
Perhaps they had some confidence that we all forgot.

When looking at these sites, I particularly love the jack-of-all-trades manner
of being that these hackers had/have.
Almost all of these guys post their poetry, their music, their life's story, and
all of their favorite things. It's a window into their souls, and their most
vulnerable places at that.
It's like everything these people are is collected in this one place, where all
of them can continue to live on. 

Along with these wonderful tidbits are their pictures. 
Each of these pictures is always so sublime, both aesthetically and in what they
represent. 
They're usually candid shots of everyday life, and I love them all.

I wanted to start this website off with a list of some cool stuff I like, kind
of like how those old internet homepages worked. Here it is!

## Me
I play the piano and a little guitar, to a pretty serious level when I was a
kid. Now I make silly music in Logic Pro X and try out different ideas I have.
What a wonderful time to be alive, when all the sounds of the world are
available to me!

I make short movies, since that is also an incredibly accessible medium now. I
have a ~25 minute short filmed last summer with some friends that I'll post here
when it's finished, and a couple of music videos shot recently. Those are my
favorite medium by far, because music and visuals go so well together.

[Derek Sivers](https://sive.rs/clothes) is my idol.
[Josh Waitzkin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?time_continue=1&v=lj1gxz5puaQ&feature=emb_logo) has recently been a close second.
Finally, [Jon Blow](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fpPRPThlbL8) means a lot to me as a person. These three take life very
seriously, stay curious, and work competitively, striving to understand fully.
They are my role models, and different aspects of each speaks to different goals
I have in my life.

My favorite musicians (in no particular order) are Leonard Cohen, Yoko Kanno,
Labi Siffre, The Mountain Goats, Billy Joel, Paul Simon, Father John Misty,
Jeffrey Lewis, Dion, Haruomi Hosono, Jonathan Richman, Lamp, Glen Campbell, John
Prine, Car Seat Headrest, Bach, Mozart, Jim Croce, and America.

My favorite movies are hard to tie down, but I really loved *The End of
Evangelion* once, when it was closer to me. The last really good movie I watched
was probably *The Wind Rises*, which made me cry tears of joy! Never had that
happen before, so I'm looking for more movies that can do that. I recently
watched *The Adolescence of Utena*. Loved the colors, but for the most part, it
wasn't my favorite.

I've been reading books a lot more recently, mostly self-help books. Recent
favorite is *The Subtle Art of Not Giving a Fuck* by Mark Manson, which was a
lot of fun to read. I also love Nietzsche, mostly *The Gay Science* and
*Twilight of the Idols*, and I read *Invisible Cities* last spring and it really
made an impression on me.
