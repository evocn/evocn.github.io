Equipment

Equipment is everything a character wears and carries. This game focuses a lot on keeping this grounded in a dramatic way.

In Bones, as in real life, if you’re planning on fighting Goblins in a dank dungeon, it’s easier to avoid a rusty knife when you don’t have a set of armor, heavy backpack, and four battle axes attached to your body.

A few keywords you should know:

  • Hands refers to how many hands it takes to carry or use equipment.
    • Most equipment which needs 2 Hands can be held in just 1 Hand in a pinch.
  • Stow refers to how much space on your body a piece of equipment takes to attach to you.
    • If not stated, equipment’s Stow value is the same as its Hands value.

Here’s how it works:

  • You can Carry equipment in your hands (so usually two small things, or one big thing).
  • You can Stow up to two Hands of equipment on your person.
  • You can wear a set of Armor, if you like.
  • A Pack carries extra gear, but while you’re carrying it, you might have some penalty, and it’s sometimes a hassle to take off quickly.

Trinkets

A trinket is a small item you can keep in your pocket. You can carry one trinket on your person for free. Every extra trinket accumulates -1 Agility.

Example Trinkets:

  • Whistle

Stow

You can attach items to your body, with straps, ropes, belt loops, and scabbards.

You can attach up to 2 weapons to your body in this way. Each item you attach to yourself in this way gives you a -1 Agility penalty.

You can stow and equip one Stowed item as a free action on your turn. Additionally, just like with your hands, you can drop Stowed items freely on your turn.

An Example:

An archer might keep his hands clear while climbing or riding a horse, with his bow and quiver attached to his body. This gives him -2 agility.

He spots an enemy! He pulls the quiver and bow off, leaving the arrows planted in the earth beside him, and nocking an arrow into his bow with both hands. Therefore, he does not have any agility penalty now.

If he decided to attach the quiver to his body, in order to keep moving, he’d keep a -1 agility penalty.

Pack

Along with your hands and Stowed items, you can carry one Pack, which can contain a number of items.

Taking an item out of a Pack takes an entire Action.

Packs Items Penalties
Sack 1 -1 Agility. Bonus Action to take it off.
Backpack 4 Half Speed. -2 Agility. Full action to take it off. -2 to Strength when climbing or otherwise pulling your own weight.
