Proposal

Why?

Violence is so misrepresented and misunderstood.

In films, stories, games, and even in the general public consciousness, what we call “violence” is a pale echo of anything real.

For instance, in film, a skilled knight might defeat four or five soldiers in battle. This is insanity! Imagine being in a room with even two enemy combatants. Think of the terror of knowing you’re outnumbered. Even as a skilled fighter.

This is the kind of simple drama I seek to create with this game.

Now, I’m not setting out to right the wrongs of society and culture. I actually just think that real-life violence is far more rich and compelling a subject than the form of violence which our media focuses on.

I think we can make a really compelling game about how violence really is. While I can’t say for certain that this game will be able to succeed at that goal, I want to give it a real, earnest effort. I want us to explore it the best we can.

Premise

I propose a set of short games, exploring a variety of topics and characters. The games involve one to three players, depending on the subject.

They take place in a single setting, in vicinity to one another, but are mostly unrelated to one another. Each game is 1-3 sessions, based on the subject.

My inspirations are the following. I want to run a game which is:

  • raw, gritty, and simultaneously sensitive, like First Blood.
  • pulp-y, quick, and mysterious, like Conan, the Barbarian.
  • honorable of the past, dark, and beautiful, like The Dying Earth.

Request to a prospective player…

In order to run this game successfully, I need you to:

  • Dedicate your time to the game for each story you take part in. This will be a weekly game.
  • Dedicate your heart and mind to exploring these concepts with us.
  • Watch / Read the above Inspirations prior to the first session.

If you cannot fulfill these precepts, then you won’t be successful in this game.
