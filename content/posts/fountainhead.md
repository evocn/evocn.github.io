I've always known what my Achilles heel is.

Over the last few years I've grown distant from people. I've avoided making
introductions, reaching out to strangers, etc.

I've had my justifications: "I'm working on something," "I don't want to
compromise myself to try to make an impression on them," and similar thoughts
come to mind.

The true reason was always twofold.
1. **Elitism** | I believed that no one could offer me anything new, and they were
   just wasting my time.
2. **Fear** | that they'll see through me, they'll see that I'm a fraud.

It comes to me in my weakest moments, this fear. It's simple, slipstream.

## Knowledge
There's an old adage, well-known:

> It's not what you know, but who you know.

I always hated this saying; I fought against it violently.
A new idea happened upon me recently that recontextualized the fight:

> Who you know *is* what you know.

Goethe said it like this: "Show me who you spend time with and I will show you
who you are."

You are the amalgamation of the people you're closest to. That much is clear.
For me, however, I don't spend that much time with people. Like I said, I
generally stick to myself, focusing on my craft.

What does that mean for what I know?

## The Fountainhead
is a book by Ayn Rand which I read recently.

Its portrayal of two characters trapped in the drama of all creation cut
straight to the heart of my fears.

Howard Roark is an architect, an uncompromising artist – the ideal man.
He works for the sake of product and practice, to glorify his own life force,
and to exert it onto the world. He relaxes completely when he doesn't work,
experiencing the sensation of life as a pure stream of idea.

Peter Keating is a parasite, a social king – everything wrong with business.
He acts in accordance with society's expectations, never fulfills his own
artistic desires (if he can even recognize such an instinct in himself), and
lives miserably, caught between desire to become his own man and the crushing
weight of social pressure.

I am Peter Keating.

In moments of fleeting vision, I transcend to become Howard Roark. Like some
Steppenwolf of both wolf and man, I try to justify myself as Roark most of the
time, but it's a rare form of expression. Usually it occurs during the process
of creation. When making a movie, game, or blog post. As for Roark's complete
relaxation, his momentary bliss, almost never.

## Reconciliation
I will always follow that which is the more eternal, the more personal. 
The one which expresses the subjective reality of my experience.

But the subjective reality I can express is smaller, it's weaker without others.
It's flawed, it forgets crucial information, and can't test theories against
other fresh ideas.

I've long used the "Do it all yourself" mentality to cover up my secret fears.
I will seek people in earnest, looking for ideas and companions seeking the same
goal I do.
